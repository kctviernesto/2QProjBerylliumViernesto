STATISTICS & NEWS

The AAPI (Asian-American and Pacific Islander) community is recently facing racist and violent attacks as the pandemic continues to take a toll on citizens, especially in countries like the United States. The "Statistics & News" Page aims to give you an idea of how much the community is suffering. Most of the reports and facts are taken from a nonprofit organization formed in 2020 called Stop AAPI Hate, whose mission, not unlike that of this website, is to offer resources to educate and report incidents related to the Stop AAPI Hate movement.

AN OVERVIEW OF THE STATISTICS

According to a report done by the Pew Research Center, about 4 in 10 U.S. adults believe "it has become more common for people to express racist views toward Asians since the pandemic began."

From March 2020 to February 2021, the reporting center of Stop AAPI Hate received a staggering total of 3795 reports. This represents only a fraction of the real total number of discriminatory hate incidents Asian-Americans face.

Verbal harassment (68.1%), shunning (20.5%), and physical assault (11.1%) make up the three largest proportions of the total incidents, according to Stop AAPI Hate's 2020â€“2021 National Report.

OTHER STATISTICS FROM REPORTS DONE BY STOP AAPI HATE

Here are some more facts from reports. The data and the answers are from the respondents who reported hate incidents to Stop AAPI Hate's reporting center. Click on the graphs to read the reports yourself!


  • People of Chinese ethnicity make up 42.2% of the respondents. Filipinos make up 7.9%.
  • 68% of the respondents are female. 29% of them are male while 2% are transgender and nonbinary.
  • 1 in 5 Asian-Americans who have experienced racism in their lives display racial trauma.
